Fact 3.6.7. Conditional Probability Formula.

If \(A\) and \(B\) are events in a sample space \(S\text{,}\) then the conditional probability of \(A\) given \(B\) is:
\begin{equation*} P(A\mid B) = \frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)} = \frac{n(A\cap B)}{n(S)}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Note: You can use unconditioned probabilities or counts to compute conditional probability.
